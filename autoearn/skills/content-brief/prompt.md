## SECTION 2: Audience Persona

**Who is this for?**
Write a 100-word persona description covering:
- Demographics and professional context
- The specific problem or question that led them to search for this content
- What they already know (don't over-explain basics)
- What they fear, want, or hope to gain from reading
- Their likely next action after consuming this content

---

## SECTION 3: Search Intent Analysis

**Primary Intent:** [Informational / Navigational / Commercial / Transactional]

**What the searcher actually wants:**
- Write 2-3 sentences describing the real question behind the keyword
- Identify whether they want a quick answer, a deep guide, a comparison, or a tool

**SERP Observations:**
- Describe the type of content currently ranking (listicle, how-to, tool pages, etc.)
- Note the average word count of top-ranking pages
- Identify any content gaps (what's missing from existing top results)

---

## SECTION 4: Recommended Angle & Hook

**Angle:** A specific perspective or framing that differentiates this piece from what already ranks.
- Not just "a guide to X" — but "why everyone is wrong about X" or "the X framework no one teaches"

**Hook concept:** How should the article open? Write 2 opening line options.

**Unique value proposition for this article:**
What will readers get here that they cannot get anywhere else currently ranking?

---

## SECTION 5: Suggested Outline

Provide a detailed H2/H3 structure:

### [H1 Title — write 2 options]

**Introduction** (target: 100-150 words)
- What to include / what to avoid

**## H2: [Section Title]** (target: XXX words)
- Key points to cover: [bullet list]
- Specific angle for this section:
- Subheadings to consider:
  - ### H3: [Subheading]
  - ### H3: [Subheading]

[Repeat for all major sections — minimum 4 H2 sections]

**Conclusion** (target: 100-150 words)
- Summarize X
- CTA: [specific action]
