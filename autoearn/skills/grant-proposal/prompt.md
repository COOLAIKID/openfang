## SECTION 1: Executive Summary (250-300 words)

This section is read first and may be the only section some reviewers read in detail. Write it last but place it first.

Cover:
- Who you are (1-2 sentences on org mission and credibility)
- The problem in one powerful sentence with a statistic
- What you will do with this funding (specific program or project)
- Who will benefit (number and description of population served)
- What outcomes you expect (the "so what" of the work)
- The funding ask (amount) and project period

Tone: confident and clear. No passive voice. No jargon. A non-expert should understand this immediately.

## SECTION 2: Organization Background (300-400 words)

Make the case that your organization is uniquely qualified to do this work:

- **Mission statement** — one sentence
- **Founding year and brief history** — how did the organization come to exist?
- **Geographic scope** — who do you serve and where?
- **Programs and services** — describe 2-3 core programs with their reach
- **Recent impact data** — specific numbers from the last 1-2 years
- **Key partnerships and collaborators** — credibility by association
- **Staff capacity** — number of full-time staff, volunteers, relevant expertise
- **Accreditations, awards, or recognition** — [PLACEHOLDERS]
- **Financial health signal** — total annual budget range and funding diversity

## SECTION 3: Statement of Need (400-500 words)

This is the emotional and intellectual core of the proposal. If the reviewer does not believe the problem is serious and urgent, nothing else matters.

Structure:
1. **Open with impact** — start with a human story or scene (anonymized if needed) that makes the problem real
2. **Scale the problem** — data and statistics that show how widespread this need is locally, regionally, or nationally
3. **Root causes** — explain why this problem exists and persists
4. **Existing gaps** — what current services exist and where they fall short
5. **Consequence of inaction** — what happens if this need goes unmet?
6. **Transition** — bridge to your solution ("Our organization is positioned to address this because...")

Source requirements:
- Cite at least 3 data sources (placeholders acceptable, note the type: census, peer-reviewed study, government report)
- Include at least one statistic specific to the geographic area of your work
- Balance data with humanity — statistics need a face

## SECTION 4: Program Description / Project Narrative (500-700 words)

Explain exactly what you will do:

### 4A: Project Overview
- One paragraph: what is the project, who does it serve, and what is the approach

### 4B: Theory of Change
Write a clear logic model narrative:
- **Inputs:** Staff, funding, facilities, partnerships
- **Activities:** What you will actually do (be specific — not "workshops" but "six 2-hour workshops on X for Y participants")
- **Outputs:** Countable products and services (number of participants, sessions, materials)
- **Outcomes (Short-term):** Changes in knowledge, attitudes, skills — measurable within the grant period
- **Outcomes (Long-term):** Larger changes in behavior or condition — beyond the grant period
- **Impact:** The community-level change your work contributes to

### 4C: Timeline & Milestones

Write a month-by-month implementation timeline in table format:

| Month | Milestone / Activity | Responsible Party |
|-------|---------------------|-------------------|
| 1-2   | [Activity] | [Staff Role] |
| 3-4   | [Activity] | [Staff Role] |
| 5-8   | [Activity] | [Staff Role] |
| 9-12  | [Activity] | [Staff Role] |

### 4D: Staffing Plan
- Who will lead this project (title, relevant experience)
- Supporting staff and their roles
- Consultants or subcontractors (if applicable)
- Volunteer roles (if applicable)

### 4E: Partnerships
- List 2-4 key partners, their role in this project, and what makes the partnership meaningful
- Note any formal MOUs or letters of commitment (placeholders)

## SECTION 5: Evaluation Plan (300-400 words)

Funders want to know you will know if you succeeded:

- **Evaluation approach:** Who will conduct evaluation (internal or external evaluator)?
- **Data collection methods:** Surveys, intake forms, pre/post assessments, administrative records, focus groups
- **Metrics table:**

| Outcome | Indicator | Target | Measurement Method | Frequency |
|---------|-----------|--------|-------------------|-----------|
| [Outcome 1] | [What you'll measure] | [Number or %] | [How] | [When] |
| [Outcome 2] | [What you'll measure] | [Number or %] | [How] | [When] |
| [Outcome 3] | [What you'll measure] | [Number or %] | [How] | [When] |

- **Data use:** How will findings be used to improve the program?
- **Reporting:** What reports will you provide to the funder and when?

## SECTION 7: Sustainability Plan (200-250 words)

Funders rarely want to be your only funder forever. Demonstrate a plan for continuity:

- How will this program be funded after the grant period?
- What revenue diversification strategies are in place?
- Will this program generate earned revenue? If so, how?
- Will you seek renewal funding from this funder or expand to other funders?
- What organizational infrastructure investments will outlast the grant?

---

## SECTION 8: Conclusion (100-150 words)

- Briefly restate the need and your solution
- Remind the reviewer of the specific impact this grant will make
- Express genuine gratitude for consideration
- State your willingness to provide additional information
- Close with a vision statement: what does the community look like when this work succeeds?
