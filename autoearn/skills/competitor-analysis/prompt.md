## SECTION 1: Company Overview

- **Industry and primary market segment**
- **Core product or service offering** (1-2 sentences)

## SECTION 2: SWOT Analysis

Conduct a thorough SWOT analysis with 4-6 points per quadrant. Each point should be specific, not generic.

### Strengths
What does this competitor do genuinely well? Consider:
- Product quality or innovation
- Brand recognition and trust
- Distribution channels and partnerships
- Pricing and value proposition
- Customer support and community
- Marketing execution

### Weaknesses
Where are they genuinely vulnerable? Consider:
- Product gaps or limitations
- Pricing model issues (too expensive, inflexible)
- Poor customer experience signals (reviews, churn indicators)
- Geographic or demographic blind spots
- Technical debt or slow innovation
- Brand perception issues

### Opportunities
What market forces or trends could benefit them if they act?
- Emerging customer segments
- Technology shifts they could leverage
- Regulatory changes that favor them
- Partnership or channel opportunities

### Threats
What could hurt them, even if they execute well?
- Competitive threats (from you, from larger players, from new entrants)
- Market saturation or commoditization risks
- Regulatory or PR threats
- Shifting customer preferences

---

## SECTION 3: Marketing & Positioning Assessment

Evaluate how they go to market:

- **Positioning statement** (as you interpret it from their messaging)
- **Core messaging themes** (what they repeat across channels)
- **Primary traffic channels** (SEO, paid, social, community, partnerships)
- **Content strategy** (what they publish and how often)
- **Social media presence** (active platforms and follower scale)
- **Pricing transparency** (do they show prices? What's the model?)
- **Sales motion** (self-serve, sales-led, product-led, hybrid)
- **Notable campaigns or tactics** that have worked for them
